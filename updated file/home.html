LIST OF OFFENSES

MAJOR OFFENSES

These are acts that do the greatest damage to the person's dignity and honor to the school the penalty for repeated commission of these offenses is dismissal from school.

A.Fighting, physical abuse of any person or conducts which threatens and/or endangers the health and safety of any person.

B. Direct assault upon a person or a member of the faculty, administration or non-teaching staff of any person vested with authority or any student.

C. Lying or giving false testimony during school investigation.

D. Slander/Utterance of offensive words that tend to cause dishonor or discredit or contempt in the name of the school (e.g. misbehavior in the public places, shoplifting, etc)

E. Stealing the school property and that of others.

F. Defacing the school places and other acts of vandalism.

G. Tampering, alteration or misuse of academic or official records (report cards, form 137, clearances, excuse letter, etc).

H. Forgery of signature of a person in authority or parent in an official communication.

I. Loitering in public places during class hours.

J. Using somebody else's ID card,

K. Exhibition and display of any obscene or pornographic magazine, pictures or videos within the school campus.

L. Gambling in any form in campus.

M. Encouraging others to violate school policies.

N. Gross misconduct, repeated disregard of school policies and regulations (major and minor)

O. Cheating/violating rules during an examination. A learner caught cheating during an examination will get zero in the said test.

MINOR OFFENSES

A. Littering

B. Staying in place other than the officially designated places during flag ceremony and other assemblies.

C. Improper behavior during flag ceremony and other assemblies.

D. Disregard for policy for haircut (for boys)

E. Wearing excessive makeup, nail polish, dyed hair, unbecoming hairstyle, etc.

F. Incomplete uniform

G. Tardiness

H. Failure to bring excuse letter from absence.

I. Petty quarrels with classmates.

J. Leaving the classroom without the teachers permission.

K. Playing harmful practical jokes.

L. Tampering ID/ wearing the ID improperly.

M. Use of mobile phones and electronic devices during classes (the teacher has the right to confiscate them when used during his/her class. Only the parent/ guardian can claim them).

N. Cutting classes

O. Loitering in any part of the school vicinity during class hours.

P. Disrespect and misbehavior.
